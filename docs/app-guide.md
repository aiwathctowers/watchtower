# Watchtower — User Guide

Watchtower is a macOS desktop app that syncs your Slack workspace to a local database and uses AI to generate insights: daily briefings, digests, tracks, and people analytics.

## Tabs & Features

### Briefings
Personalized daily briefings generated by AI. Each briefing consolidates data from all other pipelines (digests, tracks, people cards) into a single morning overview tailored to your role.

A briefing contains five sections:

**Needs Attention** — Items requiring your immediate focus: updated tracks, new red flags from people cards. Each item shows priority, reason, and links to its source (track, digest, or person). Click to navigate.

**Your Day** — Active tracks for the day. Shows priority and current status. If no tracks exist yet, a hint is shown that tracks are auto-generated from digests. Click to navigate to the track.

**What Happened** — Notable events from recent channel digests: decisions, key discussions, important topics. Shows channel name, item type, and importance. Click to open the digest.

**Team Pulse** — People signals: activity spikes or drops, new red flags, highlights, conflicts. Click to navigate to the person's card.

**Coaching Corner** — Communication and process recommendations based on your interactions. Categories include communication, delegation, conflict, and process tips.

The list panel shows briefings by date with unread indicator (blue dot), attention count, and task count. Selecting a briefing marks it as read. Briefings are generated once per day after the configured hour (default: 8:00 AM, configurable in Settings via `briefing.hour`).

### AI Chat
Chat with Claude about your workspace. The bot can query your Slack database to answer questions about messages, activity, decisions, and people.
- Model selector: switch between Sonnet, Haiku, and Opus
- Multi-turn conversations with session memory
- Quick prompts: "What happened today?", "Any decisions?", "Summarize activity"
- Chat history sidebar (toggle with button or Cmd+N for new chat)
- Bot can generate Slack deep links to specific messages

### Tracks
Auto-generated narrative summaries of ongoing initiatives and projects across your Slack workspace. Tracks are created and updated automatically by the AI pipeline — they group related digest topics into living stories that evolve over time.

**How tracks work:** After each sync, the tracks pipeline collects unlinked topics and decisions from recent digests, compares them against existing tracks, and either creates new tracks or updates existing ones. Each track tells a narrative story about an initiative — who's involved, what happened, what the current status is.

**Updates:** When a track is updated after you've read it, it appears in the "Updates" section at the top of the list with an orange badge. Opening a track automatically marks it as read and cascades read status to its linked digests.

**Priority:** High (red), Medium (orange), Low (blue). Click the priority badge on a track to change it.

Each track shows:
- **Current Status** — highlighted card summarizing the latest state
- **Narrative** — full story of the initiative
- **Timeline** — chronological list of events with dates and channels
- **Participants** — people involved with role badges (driver=green, reviewer=blue, blocker=red)
- **Key Messages** — quoted Slack messages with authors, timestamps, and links to open in Slack
- **Related Digests** — expandable section showing linked digests via source references. Each digest can be expanded (DisclosureGroup) to see its summary, decisions, and feedback buttons
- **Tags** — topic tags for categorization
- **Channels** — which Slack channels the track spans (clickable deep links)

**Actions:** Change priority, rate quality with thumbs up/down, chat with AI about a specific track.

### Digests
AI-generated summaries of channel activity. Two sub-views:

**Digests** — Per-channel summaries, daily rollups, and weekly trends. Each digest shows: summary, extracted topics, ongoing topics with status, decisions. Rate quality with thumbs up/down.

**Decisions** — Flat list of all decisions across digests. Shows importance level (editable to train AI), channel, author, date. Expandable for full context.

### People
Team member profiles generated by AI from Slack activity analysis.

Each person card shows: summary, communication style, decision role, accomplishments, red flags, highlights, activity hours chart, and history across weeks.

**Your card** has extra tabs: Overview + Connections (manager, reports, peers — editable).

**Team Summary** shows overall team health metrics and dynamics.

### Statistics
Channel analytics and workspace hygiene tools. The tab uses a sub-tab layout (currently one tab: Channels).

**Channels tab** — Table of all synced channels with activity metrics and AI-readiness indicators.

Columns: channel name (clickable Slack deep link), total messages, your messages, bot message percentage, mention count, last activity, status icons (muted/favorite/archived).

**Toolbar:** filter picker (All / Muted / Favorites / Recommendations), sort order (Messages / Name / Bot % / Activity), and channel search.

**Recommendations** — Heuristic-based suggestions displayed above the table:
- **Suggest Mute** — channels with 70%+ bot traffic, or high-volume channels (50+ messages) where you have no messages and no mentions. Click "Mute" to exclude the channel from AI pipelines.
- **Suggest Leave** — channels where you have never posted, or where your last activity was 30+ days ago. Skipped for DMs, favorites, and watched channels. Click "Mute" to stop processing.
- **Suggest Favorite** — channels where you post frequently (10+ messages) and are mentioned often (3+ mentions), or channels on your watch list. Click "Favorite" to prioritize in analysis.

**Actions per channel row:**
- **Mute/Unmute for AI** — toggles whether AI pipelines (digests, tracks, briefings) process this channel. Muted channels are skipped during analysis, reducing noise and token costs.
- **Favorite/Unfavorite** — marks a channel as important for prioritized analysis.

### Search
Full-text search across all synced Slack messages. Shows matching messages with channel, author, text snippet, and timestamp.

### Usage
**Progress tab** — Live pipeline status (sync, digests, tracks, people) with progress bars and ETA.
**Usage tab** — Historical token consumption and costs by date, model, and feature. Navigate between days.

### Training
Fine-tune AI prompts based on your feedback. Shows quality score, feedback stats, prompt editor with version history, importance corrections, and tuning controls.

## Settings

**General:** Sync interval, workers, history depth, digest model/language, briefing hour, Claude CLI path.
**Profile:** Your role, team, manager, reports, peers, starred channels/people.
**Notifications:** Decision alerts, daily summaries, quiet hours.
**Daemon:** Start/stop the background sync daemon, view status.
**Training:** Prompt editor, feedback stats, tuning.
**Logs:** Live daemon logs with filtering.
**Data:** Storage info, regenerate AI data, delete everything.

## Background Processes

Watchtower runs a daemon (`watchtower sync --detach`) that periodically syncs Slack data. After each sync, AI pipelines run automatically:
1. **Digest pipeline** — generates per-channel summaries (MAP phase for people signals)
2. **Tracks pipeline** — auto-creates and updates tracks from unlinked digest topics, then injects track context into rollup digests
3. **Rollup digests** — generates daily/weekly cross-channel rollups (track-aware)
4. **People pipeline** — builds team member profiles from extracted situations (once per day)
5. **Briefing pipeline** — generates a personalized daily briefing from all of the above (once per day, after the configured hour)

## Key Concepts

- **Running context** — AI maintains a "memory" per channel, carrying forward active topics, recent decisions, and open questions between digest runs
- **Situations** — Extracted interaction patterns (conflicts, collaborations, bottlenecks) used to build people cards
- **Feedback loop** — Your thumbs up/down ratings and importance corrections improve AI quality over time through prompt tuning
- **Starred items** — Star channels and people to prioritize them in analysis and filtering
- **Muted channels** — Channels excluded from AI processing (digests, tracks, briefings). Use the Statistics tab to mute noisy or bot-heavy channels and reduce token costs
